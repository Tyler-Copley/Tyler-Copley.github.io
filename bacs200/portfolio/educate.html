How To Help Every Child Fulfil Their Potential: Carol Dweck There are two mindsets, growth and fixed, that students fall into. Fixed believes that you have a set amount of talent making effort unnecessary. While growth is more or less the opposite, thinking effort pays off. These mindsets can be set through different forms of praise and how others handle their achievements.
Smile Or Die: Barbara Ehrenreich The need for people to be positive can be seen as delusional, which is always a mistake. One would think over positivity could lead to high productivity, but people are hardwired to be vigilant and on guard. This positive need can also be described as a social goal that can guide people to predictable decision making.
Re-Imagining Work: Dave Coplin Most of the workforce aren't not happy with their current job. Technology can be the reason for unhappy/disengaged workers as being unable to use current technology decreases productivity. Work enviorment also can lead to lesser productivity, being in a or surrounded by negativity weakens the moral of workers.
